Case studies on trending events

This case study is centered around trending events and falls under a holistic SEO approach, which makes it harder to follow compared to simpler cases. The example shows a pattern where traffic remains stable for a period, then experiences a sharp increase due to a trending event like university exams. The key observation is that after such jumps, rankings do not necessarily drop; instead, they can continue to grow over time. This pattern repeats across similar projects, where traffic has been multiplied multiple times within a year by leveraging the same type of trend. The project also scaled to over 100,000 clicks per day, and even after initial spikes, the growth continued rather than declining. Additional traffic sources like Discover, images, and video contributed to turning the site into a broader media property.

The impact of artificial intelligence

Artificial intelligence was primarily used for translation to speed up content production. The core strategy, however, revolves around topical authority, which is influenced by historical data, topical coverage, and cost of retrieval. Over time, as impressions and clicks increase, search engines begin to trust the site more, leading to higher crawl frequency and prioritized indexing. Instead of focusing only on high-value money keywords, the approach emphasizes ranking for semantically related but slightly distant topics first. This builds historical data, which can later be directed toward core pages through internal linking.

In this case, the business focused on transcription services, and related topics like YouTube transcription naturally connected to areas like video downloading and streaming. These were structured into topical maps, separating monetization-focused content from authority-building content. Another example involved ranking for university exams, which tied into course sales, occupations, and academic branches. These interconnected topics strengthen each other when properly mapped.

Analyzing traffic fluctuations

Traffic data from tools like Ahrefs and Semrush shows consistent growth patterns when this strategy is applied. The key takeaway is that historical data becomes the differentiating factor over time. Once a site gains enough visibility and engagement, search engines begin to evaluate it differently, increasing its overall importance.

Insights from the Ben Gomes patent

A key concept comes from work associated with Ben Gomes. Instead of defining duplicate content purely by text similarity, duplication is evaluated based on queries. Multiple documents may overlap depending on the query, and search engines determine which sections of which pages best answer specific intents.

This is why content should not attempt to provide the best answer everywhere on a page. The strongest, most complete answers should be placed in the most relevant sections, while supporting content should guide users through internal links. This structure improves both indexing and relevance. Page rank and URL signals also influence which page becomes canonical, sometimes overriding explicit canonical tags.

Understanding the growth of clicks

Patience plays a major role in SEO success. In this project, work began in September 2023, starting from around 400 daily clicks and growing to over 100,000 per day, with additional traffic from other sources pushing totals even higher. Growth is not always linear, and there are frequent drops of 40–50 percent. However, maintaining the same strategy through these fluctuations is essential. Changing direction too early would have prevented long-term growth.

The role of Jeffrey Dean in search evolution

Another important perspective comes from Jeff Dean. His work explains how documents are clustered using mechanisms like sliding windows. Search systems group similar documents and serve the most relevant ones efficiently. If a page’s structure lacks contextual alignment between headings, subheadings, and supporting content, it may not be properly clustered or surfaced.

This explains why some pages receive very few impressions. The system may briefly evaluate them but moves on if stronger candidates exist. Consistency in structure and context increases the chances of being selected during these evaluations.

Ranking in search as a web entity

Modern search systems increasingly rank entities rather than just pages. Instead of focusing only on indexing documents, search engines evaluate how often a brand or entity is mentioned, in what context, and with what sentiment. This requires broader topical coverage, including comparisons, alternatives, and competitor-related content. Creating sections that directly compare your offering with others helps establish stronger entity-level relevance.

Pavel Klimanov’s research findings

Research shared by Pavel Klimanov highlights how systems distinguish between human-written and AI-generated content. These models analyze patterns, structure, and characteristics to determine content origin. The practical takeaway is to prioritize originality and avoid repetitive patterns. AI is best used as a tool for analysis and augmentation rather than direct content generation. Tracking unique co-occurrences and integrating them with search data can improve performance while maintaining content quality.

The importance of community in SEO

The broader SEO community plays a role in refining and validating these approaches. Case studies, shared results, and collaborative insights help evolve strategies over time. Continuous learning and adaptation remain essential, especially as search systems and AI capabilities evolve.

Final thoughts

The key ideas across this case study are consistency, structured topical coverage, and patience. Growth comes from building historical data, aligning content with query intent, and maintaining a long-term strategy despite short-term fluctuations. Instead of reacting to every update, focusing on a well-defined plan and executing it over multiple core updates leads to sustainable results.
