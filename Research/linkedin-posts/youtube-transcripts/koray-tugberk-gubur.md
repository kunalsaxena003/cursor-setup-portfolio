The impact of artificial intelligence

In this project, we used artificial intelligence mainly for translation. This helped us increase execution speed. However, the real advantage came from historical data and topical authority.

Topical authority is shaped by historical data, coverage, and retrieval cost. As you can see, rankings increased multiple times over different timeframes—three months, six months, and yearly comparisons all show strong growth.

When your site gains more impressions and clicks, search engines begin to trust it more. This leads to higher crawl frequency and prioritized indexing. At that point, search engines run more advanced algorithms on your site.

The key takeaway is that instead of targeting only money keywords, you should also target semantically related but slightly distant topics. This helps you build historical data first. Then, through internal linking, you can transfer that authority to your core pages.

For example, in this project, the main business was transcription. But we also covered related topics like YouTube downloads, recording, and streaming. These topics are connected semantically, even if they are not directly tied to monetization.

In a topical map, core topics represent monetization, while auxiliary topics support authority building. Authority flows from auxiliary sections to core sections through internal linking.

Analyzing traffic fluctuations

You can analyze performance using tools like Ahrefs or SEMrush. Each tool shows slightly different data, but patterns remain consistent.

One important concept comes from a patent by Ben Gomes. Instead of defining duplicate content strictly by text, it evaluates duplication based on queries. This means multiple documents can overlap partially depending on query intent.

For example, two pages may share only partial overlap for one query but be completely duplicate for another. Search engines evaluate which document is most relevant based on passages, headings, and context.

This is especially relevant for AI-driven systems that retrieve multiple sources and generate answers by combining them.

In my methodology, I don’t try to give the best answer everywhere. Instead, I provide the best answer in the most relevant section. Less important sections act as supporting content and link to the primary page.

This approach improves indexing and content hierarchy. It also ensures that search engines understand which page should rank for which query.

Understanding the growth of clicks

Patience is critical. We have been working on this project since September 2023. It started with around 400 clicks per day and now exceeds 100,000 daily clicks.

Growth is not linear. You will see fluctuations, drops, and spikes. Sometimes traffic may drop by 40–50%, but if your strategy is correct, it will recover and grow further.

If you change your strategy every time traffic drops, you will never reach long-term growth. Instead, you need to trust your plan and continue execution.

If you follow a consistent approach, you should evaluate results over multiple core updates, not short-term changes. If after several updates there is no improvement, then adjustments may be needed.

The role of Jeffrey Dean in search evolution

Another important concept comes from Jeffrey Dean. He explains how documents are clustered using a sliding window approach.

Search engines group similar documents and then select the most relevant ones to serve. If your content structure is not consistent—if headings, subheadings, and text do not align—your page may not be properly clustered.

Even if it is indexed, it may not be selected as a strong candidate. The system does not keep evaluating endlessly. It selects the best available result quickly.

That is why some pages receive very few impressions. The system tests them occasionally to see if they can gather more data.

To succeed, your content must be structurally consistent and contextually aligned. Titles, headings, and body content must reinforce the same topic clearly.

Ranking in search as a web entity

Search engines increasingly rank entities, not just pages. This means your brand must be recognized as an entity within a topic.

Entity ranking depends on mentions, context, and sentiment across the web. It is not just about your own site but also how others reference you.

In my approach, I always include comparison and alternative sections. This allows me to rank for competitor-related queries and position my entity within the broader ecosystem.

You need to build a presence where your brand is consistently associated with relevant topics.

Pavel Klimanov's research findings

Another important topic is AI content detection. Research shows that systems can differentiate between human-written and AI-generated content using various signals.

There are measurable characteristics that define originality, structure, and uniqueness. Instead of relying entirely on AI-generated content, it is better to use AI as an analytical tool.

Focus on originality. Measure how much of your content is unique in terms of named entities, relationships, and co-occurrences. This helps improve performance and maintain content quality.

Understanding these patterns allows you to create content that performs well in both traditional search and AI-driven systems.
